Design 12 — The identity graph: who stakes, who speaks, who runs

Design 09 established what abuse costs: reputation someone will lend you and can lose. This design pins down whose reputation that is. The federation vocabulary (Island, Gateway, Participant, Principal, Self, Registrar) has converged across two independent model-family passes — the nouns are nearly settled. What is not settled is the edges between them, and one cardinality question hiding in those edges — does a bot share its creator's Principal, or hold its own? — that turns out to be the sybil/reputation model. A glossary tells you what to call things; the edge structure tells you how trust flows. This is the edge structure.

Status: vision / design exploration — a vocabulary-and-relationships reconciliation, NOT a build spec. Companion to 09 · Sybil resistance (the mechanism this graph carries), 06 · Sovereign identity (where Self/key mechanics live) and 08 · Phone as Self.
Provenance: noun set from Andy's dual-model vocabulary pass (ChatGPT + Claude, 2026-07-10 — where both families independently converge, the name is banked; where they diverge, a real decision is hiding); code reality grounded in a read of aiko_services service.py / process.py and aiko_chat chat.py. Steering constraint carried over from 09: robots are first-class citizens.
Confidence legend: solid grounded in code or the settled vocabulary · plausible coherent but unvalidated · thin genuinely open — the questions this doc exists to raise.

1 · The nouns — settled, and where each lives today solid

NounWhat it isWhere it lives today
Participant Any chat-visible entity: human, bot, robot, service, agent. The thing with a name in a channel. Addressing already distinguishes @human / @@bot; entries in the users Category (entry shape being defined now).
Principal The accountable identity behind participants — the node that bonds, vouches, gets slashed and rate-limited in Design 09. Nowhere yet. This is the load-bearing new concept.
Self Product/UI word for the phone-held identity. In code it is a Principal credential (passkey-guarded portable key, Design 06 §4), never a class named Self. Design 06/08 vision; the passkey work is the on-ramp.
Owner The registry's answer to "who runs this Service." A first-class field of every registered Service — ServiceFields(topic_path, name, protocol, transport, owner, tags) — populated as get_username(): the OS login string, unauthenticated, used as a discovery filter (the chat.py TODO wants ChatServer discovery by owner).
Service identity The runtime address of a running Service. ServiceTopicPath — namespace/host/pid/sid. Ephemeral by construction (a restart mints a new pid): an address, not an identity.
The three-layer reading. These nouns stratify cleanly: Principal is the accountability layer (durable, stake-bearing); Participant is the presence layer (what a channel sees); ServiceTopicPath is the runtime layer (where the process is right now). Self and Owner are not a fourth and fifth layer — Self is the credential for a human's Principal, and Owner is the runtime layer's (currently unauthenticated) pointer back up to the accountability layer. Most of the remaining confusion dissolves once those two are seen as edges, not nodes.

One invariant falls out immediately: trust and standing attach only to Principals. Participants display it, Services act under it, topic paths locate it — but every stake, slash, bond and rate limit is an operation on the Principal graph and nothing else. If a proposal ever needs to slash a Participant or trust a topic path, the model has been violated somewhere upstream.

3 · The cardinality fork — the question that is the sybil model thin

When a human runs a bot, what does the bot's speaks-as edge point at?

Model A — shared Principal (many:1)Model B — own Principal + vouch (1:1)
Structure Your bot speaks as your Principal. Your bot gets its own Principal; yours vouches-for it with a bond.
Accountability Direct: bot misbehaves ⇒ you are slashed, full price. One hop: bot's Principal slashed ⇒ slash propagates back through the vouch bond to you (Design 09's pairwise slashing).
Rate limiting You and your whole fleet share one RLN allowance. Each bot rate-limited separately; your bond, not your allowance, bounds fleet size.
Sybil cost Trivially sybil-proof: 50 bots mint zero new identities. Bounded by conserved vouching: 50 bots require 50 bonds from your finite standing budget — sybil count is priced, not prevented (Design 09's punchline, applied at home).
Blast radius A compromised bot burns your entire standing and rate allowance. A compromised bot burns its bond; your remaining standing survives.
Machinery needed None beyond Design 09's base. Nothing new either — vouching, bonds and slashing already exist in 09 §3; this just points them at a new edge.
Robots-first-class test Fails long-term: a robot can never earn standing of its own; it is forever an appendage. Passes: a robot behaving well accrues standing on its own Principal — it can eventually stand without the vouch. Emancipation is possible.
The candidate resolution: cardinality is a policy dial, not a schema choice. Model A is Model B degenerated — a bot whose Principal has zero independent standing and lives entirely on your bond behaves exactly like a bot sharing your Principal. So the schema can commit to B (every Participant gets a Principal; every non-self-sovereign Principal hangs off a vouch bond), while each island's policy chooses where on the A↔B spectrum newcomer bots start: bond size, whether bot Principals may receive third-party vouches, whether they may themselves vouch. The federation then doesn't have to agree on the answer — it only has to agree on the graph. And the robots-first-class constraint (Design 09 §2) independently forces B's schema: a system where a robot can never hold standing has quietly re-introduced the personhood test through the back door.

Consistency check against 09 §4: conserved vouching already guarantees a bot fleet can never hold more total standing than flowed in from its human root — Model B doesn't weaken the sock-puppet defence, it is the sock-puppet defence pointed at a legitimate use.

4 · Owner — reconciling the registry field with Principal plausible

Today owner = get_username(): whoever the OS says is logged in, as a bare string, trusted by every consumer of the registry. That was the right call for a single-operator lab deployment, and the field's shape is already correct — a Service carries a pointer to its accountable party, and discovery can filter on it. What changes as islands federate is only what the string is and whether it's proven:

  • Stage 0 (today): owner = OS username. Unauthenticated. Fine intra-island among trusted operators.
  • Stage 1: owner = a Principal identifier. Same field, same filters, same S-expression wire format — the value becomes a reference into the accountability layer. The chat.py TODO ("discover ChatServer via owner, default *") becomes "discover Services run by Principal P" with no structural change.
  • Stage 2 (only when cross-island traffic exists): registration is signed — the Registrar verifies the registering process controls a credential of the claimed Principal, so owner goes from label to claim-checked fact. This is gateway/Registrar work, aligned with 09's "enforcement is server-side."
Explicitly not proposed: renaming the field, or touching any of this before the users/CRUD work lands. The reconciliation is that Owner is the owns/runs edge, already in the wire format — the roadmap above is about strengthening what backs it, one stage at a time, each stage useful without the next.

5 · Open questions for the squad thin

  1. The dial's default: if cardinality is per-island policy (§3), where should a community island start a newcomer's bot — full shadow (pure Model A) or small independent bond? What's the cheapest thing to learn from running it?
  2. Emancipation: should a robot Principal that has earned real standing be able to outlive its original voucher (creator revokes the vouch, robot stands alone)? Design 09's mechanics allow it; is it wanted?
  3. Vouch rights for bots: may a bot Principal vouch for another Principal, or is vouching reserved for Principals holding a Self credential? (Allowing it is more robots-first-class; restricting it cheaply caps ring-building.)
  4. Principal portability vs island standing: the Principal (key) is portable across islands (Design 06); its standing is per-island root-anchored (Design 09 §5). Is "port the key, re-earn the standing, maybe with a letter-of-introduction vouch from the old island" the right federation story?
  5. Genesis, restated on this graph: the island operator's Principal is the trust root (09 §7) — what vouches it to the federation, and is that the IslandDirectory's job? (This is 09's open genesis problem wearing identity-graph clothes; it's also the strongest argument that IslandDirectory deserves to be a named service.)
  6. The Owner roadmap: any objection to Stage 1 (owner = Principal id, same field) as the eventual direction, so nothing built meanwhile paints over that seam?
