The rejection landmines — why chat apps specifically get bounced

A generic app sails through where a chat app fails. These three are the difference, and all three are real work that doesn't exist yet:

1 — User-generated content needs moderation (Apple 1.2 / Google UGC)

Any app where users post content others see must ship: a report mechanism, a block mechanism, a EULA with zero tolerance for objectionable content/users, and a commitment to act on reports within 24h. Missing this is the single most common chat-app rejection. → Task #4.

2 — Account creation demands account deletion (Apple 5.1.1(v))

Aiko has register/login, so it must offer in-app account deletion. There is currently no delete path at all. → Task #3.

3 — Data disclosure must match behaviour (App Privacy / Data Safety)

The privacy labels you declare are checked against what the app actually collects. A chat app collects account identity + message content — declare it accurately or face a post-review takedown.
